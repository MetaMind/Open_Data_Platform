# Go-To-Market Focus Guide (MetaMind)

## Objective
Define where MetaMind should compete, who should buy first, and what message converts.

## 1) Positioning (non-negotiable)
MetaMind should be positioned as a **cross-engine query intelligence control plane**, not as a warehouse replacement.

Positioning statement:
"MetaMind gives enterprises one control layer for routing, governance, and optimization across mixed data engines."

## 2) Ideal customer profile (ICP)
Primary ICP:
- Mid-large enterprises with 2+ query engines (e.g., warehouse + lakehouse + legacy DB)
- Teams suffering from cost unpredictability and inconsistent query behavior
- Organizations with strict governance/compliance and multi-team data ownership

Secondary ICP:
- Platform teams building internal data platforms for many consuming teams

## 3) High-value pain points to sell
- Rising cloud query costs without clear optimization governance
- Latency inconsistency across business-critical dashboards and jobs
- Vendor lock-in risk and low portability of optimization logic
- Fragmented policy/observability across engine silos

## 4) Core value pillars
1. **Cost control across engines**
2. **Consistent performance governance**
3. **Lower lock-in via control-plane architecture**
4. **Central visibility and policy enforcement**

## 5) Product packaging (simple)
- `Foundation`: health, query API, history, metrics, basic routing
- `Control`: policy/firewall, cache controls, tenant governance
- `Adaptive`: synthesis/learning, advanced tuning and recommendations

## 6) Proof points required in every deal
- % reduction in query cost (target: 15-30% in pilot scope)
- % reduction in p95 latency variance
- Reduction in incident/debug time using centralized logs/metrics
- Time-to-integrate for one engine + one business workload

## 7) Pilot-first sales motion
Pilot shape (4-6 weeks):
1. Pick 1 business domain and 2 representative workloads
2. Baseline current cost/latency/incident metrics
3. Route through MetaMind in controlled rollout
4. Compare before vs after
5. Convert pilot to platform expansion plan

## 8) Messaging by audience
- CIO/CTO: strategic control, portability, risk reduction
- Data platform lead: operational consistency and fewer cross-engine fires
- FinOps: measurable spend governance
- Analytics leaders: more predictable dashboard/query SLAs

## 9) Competitive framing
Avoid: "We are better than Snowflake/Databricks."
Use: "We maximize value across your existing stack and reduce single-vendor dependency."

## 10) Top 5 GTM risks
1. Wrong category framing (replacement vs overlay)
2. Weak quantified ROI evidence
3. Slow onboarding and complex integration
4. Enterprise trust gaps (stability/security/support)
5. Over-promising AI value before operational basics are solid

## 11) GTM success metrics
- Pilot-to-paid conversion rate
- Time-to-first-value (days)
- Net cost savings in first 90 days
- Expansion rate across teams/engines
- Renewal + referenceability
